Science fiction
Explore

Science fiction is a genre of speculative fiction, which typically deals with imaginative and futuristic concepts such as advanced science and technology, space exploration, time travel, parallel universes, and extraterrestrial life. It is related to fantasy, horror, and superhero fiction and contains many subgenres.

Fantasy
Explore

Fantasy books are typically about magic or other abstract, supernatural phenomenon. The plot and setting are usually supernatural. It often features mythological and magical characters. It sometimes also involves creatures who are not human beings. Creatures like goblins, elves, dwarfs, fantasy is a vast and ever-evolving genre. It straddles between many different sub-genres.

Horror
Explore

Horror books can make their readers scared and frightened. It startles its readers and leaves them with a feeling of horror and terror. Horror books are usually fictitious prose that shocks and frightens their readers. It generally leaves an eerie and frightening atmosphere. Horror books incite the emotion of their readers. Also, horror books portray intense moods, emotions, settings, and tone. These come together to produce a sense of heightened disgust and fright. They are sometimes a little bit too realistic.

Romance
Explore

Romance books are typically books that incite an erotic, love feeling. The plot of these types of books is a romantic relationship between two people. They are usually fictitious. The general belief is that women make up the majority of romance book readers. Some romance books do not even contain a distinct storyline but only involve romantic sexual representations. Romance books usually leave the heart warm. Examples of romance books are Fifty shades of grey by E.L James, Pride, and Prejudice by Jane Austen, Outlander by Diana Gabaldon.

Thriller And Suspense
Explore

Thriller and suspense books usually focus on the fear of a crime event that is yet to happen in the book. Thriller and suspense books generally reveal a bad character way early enough into the book and leave the main character to stop the evil that should happen. It builds up suspense and thrills the readers of the book. The main character does not just aim to prevent evil from being done by the villain, but he also seeks to destroy him to save his own life. Thrillers make use of deception and cliffhangers to introduce suspense.
